# GrabFood Group Order: The Easiest Way to Order Office & Team Lunches

Ordering lunch or coffee for the whole team used to mean one person collecting cash, tracking who ordered what, and hoping the math added up. **GrabFood Group Order** changes that: one person starts the order, everyone joins with their own choices, and the app handles splitting the bill. For workers and employees, it’s one of the simplest ways to run a team lunch or coffee round.

Here’s how it works, why teams use it, and how you can get the most out of it—including where to point your group when you want quality coffee in BSD.

## What Is GrabFood Group Order?

GrabFood Group Order is a feature that lets **up to 10 people** order from the same restaurant or merchant in one go. One person acts as the **host**: they create the order and share a link or QR code. Everyone else opens the link, adds their own items, and the host submits a single order. No more screenshots, spreadsheets, or “who had the extra rice?”

It’s built for situations like:

- Office lunch runs
- Team coffee rounds
- Department or project celebrations
- Regular lunch groups that order together

Because everyone orders through the same session, the platform can apply **group discounts** that get better as more people join—so it pays to get the whole team in.

## How to Start a Group Order (Step by Step)

If you’re the one organising lunch or coffee, you’re the host. The flow is straightforward:

1. **Open GrabFood** and choose your restaurant or café (e.g. search for the place you want in BSD or your area).
2. **Tap “Group Order”** (or the equivalent option in the order flow).
3. **Set a deadline** so people know when to add their items—e.g. “Order closes at 12:00”.
4. **Share the link or QR code** in your team chat (WhatsApp, Slack, etc.). Colleagues open it and add their own items.
5. **Review the order** before placing it. You can see who ordered what and how the total and discounts look.
6. **Choose how to pay**: you pay for everyone, split equally, or everyone pays for their own order (when supported). Then place the order.

Once the order is placed, tracking and delivery work like a normal GrabFood order—one delivery, one point of contact.

## Why Use Group Order for Office Lunches?

**Saves time**  
No more one person taking orders in a chat, then placing 5 separate orders or one huge manual order. One link, everyone adds their own items, one checkout.

**Fair splitting**  
Split bill options mean no awkward “I’ll pay you back later” or guessing who had what. The app can split by each person’s items or equally, depending on what the host selects.

**Better discounts**  
Group Order often comes with **discounts that scale with group size** (e.g. up to around 15% for larger groups). Bigger group, bigger saving—useful for daily lunch or weekly coffee runs.

**Less admin**  
No spreadsheets, no chasing payments for last week’s lunch. Everything stays inside the app.

**Clear deadline**  
The host sets a cutoff time, so the kitchen gets one consolidated order and colleagues know exactly when to add their items. That helps with planning and avoids “too late” add-ons.

## Tips for Running a Smooth Team Order

- **Send the link early** so people have time to choose. For lunch, sending the link in the morning with a clear deadline (e.g. 11:30) works well.
- **Remind once** close to the deadline so stragglers don’t miss out.
- **Pick a go-to spot** for recurring orders (e.g. same café every Friday). Familiar menus and delivery times make it faster for everyone.
- **Use Group Order for coffee runs** as well as lunch—many cafés and coffee shops on GrabFood support it, so one person can run the round without handling cash or orders manually.

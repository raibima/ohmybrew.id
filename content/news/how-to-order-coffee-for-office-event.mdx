# How to Order Coffee for Your Office Event (Jakarta)

Ordering coffee for an office event—whether it’s a town hall, product launch, or Friday team ritual—doesn’t have to mean settling for whatever the nearest café can drop off. If you want **event coffee service** or **office coffee catering** that actually fits your headcount, budget, and vibe, a bit of planning and the right vendor make all the difference. Here’s a practical way to do it, especially if you’re in **Jakarta**.

## What kind of coffee setup do you need?

Roughly three formats cover most cases:

1. **Barista-led coffee bar** — A pop-up bar (or corner) at your venue with one or more baristas making drinks to order. Best when you want a “wow” moment and have 40–150+ guests.
2. **Pre-batched and delivered** — Ready-to-pour cold coffee (or hot in dispensers) delivered to your office. Great for internal meetings, workshops, or smaller groups where you don’t need a full bar.
3. **Branded coffee corner** — A dedicated spot with signage and menu boards, often with barista service, for conferences, launches, or networking events where coffee is part of the experience.

Your choice depends on headcount, budget, and whether you want made-to-order drinks or a simpler self-serve setup.

## What to decide before you reach out

- **Date and time window** — When does the event start and end? When do you want coffee available?
- **Headcount** — Even a range (e.g. 50–80) helps vendors propose the right package.
- **Rough budget** — Per-head or total. You don’t need a final number; “mid-range” or “premium but not over the top” is enough to get options.
- **Location** — Full address and any access or setup notes (e.g. lobby, pantry, outdoor area).
- **Preferences** — More black coffee vs. milk-based? Any dietary or branding needs (e.g. branded cups, signage)?

## How to get a proposal (no online checkout)

Most **coffee catering** for offices and events is customized, so you won’t usually see a fixed “add to cart” price. Instead:

1. **Email or WhatsApp** the vendor with the details above.
2. They’ll suggest one or two formats (e.g. “Office Coffee Bar with 2 baristas” or “Pre-batched for 30 people”).
3. You agree on menu, timing, and price; they confirm and show up on the day.

That’s it. No spreadsheets, no guessing—just a short back-and-forth so the setup matches your event.

## Event coffee package options: what to ask for

When you’re comparing **event coffee package options**, ask:

- **Menu** — Do they offer signature drinks, black coffee, and classics? Can they adjust sweetness or strength?
- **Service style** — Full barista bar, self-serve from dispensers, or a mix?
- **Minimums and capacity** — Minimum headcount? Maximum they can handle in your time window?
- **Setup and cleanup** — Who brings equipment? Who clears at the end?
- **Pricing** — Per head, per hour, or all-in? What’s included (cups, napkins, etc.)?

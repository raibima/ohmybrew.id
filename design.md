## Oh My Brew – Brand & Design Guidelines

### Brand Overview

**Brand essence**: Playful, cozy, slightly mischievous coffee brand with a bold, handcrafted feel.  
**Core idea**: Specialty coffee that doesn’t take itself too seriously—warm, welcoming, a bit quirky (like a cat that owns the café).  
**Visual personality**: Hand-drawn, imperfect, bold type; strong red accents; soft, light backgrounds; simple, high-contrast shapes.

---

## 1. Logo & Wordmark

### 1.1 Primary Logo

- **Main lockup**:  
  - Text: `Oh My Brew` in a playful, hand-drawn style, stacked on three lines.  
  - Character: A black cat integrated into the composition (for example, hanging from the type or interacting with it).
- **Primary colors**:
  - Text: **Red** (`#AC0000`)
  - Character: **Black** (`#000000`)
  - Background: **Light background** (`#F2F0F6`)

### 1.2 Logo Variations

- **Full-color on light** (preferred):
  - Use the red wordmark on the light background with the black cat.
- **Full-color on dark**:
  - Background: Black (`#000000`)
  - Wordmark: White or very light neutral (see color section)
  - Cat: White or outlined in white for legibility.
- **Single-color logo**:
  - For embossing, stamps, or low-cost printing:
    - Use a 1-color version (all black or all red) with simplified shapes.

### 1.3 Clear Space & Minimum Size

- **Clear space**:
  - Maintain at least the height of the letter `O` (or equivalent) as padding on all sides of the logo.
- **Minimum size**:
  - Digital: Don’t display the logo smaller than **64 px** in width.
  - Print: Avoid sizes under **20 mm** in width to maintain legibility of the cat and lettering.

### 1.4 Incorrect Usage

Never:
- Distort or stretch the logo.
- Change the red to other hues outside the agreed palette.
- Add drop shadows, bevels, or other heavy effects.
- Place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without sufficient contrast.
- Separate the cat element from the wordmark in a way that confuses brand recognition.

## 2. Color System

### 2.1 Core Palette

- **Oh My Brew Red**: `#AC0000`  
  - **Usage**: Primary brand color. Call-to-action buttons, logo text, key headings, highlights.
- **Light Mist (Background)**: `#F2F0F6`  
  - **Usage**: Default app background, printed menu backgrounds, UI surfaces, cards.
- **Night Black**: `#000000`  
  - **Usage**: Primary body text, outlines, cat character, icons, strong contrast elements.
- **Electric Brew Blue**: `#1D0FCB`  
  - **Usage**: Secondary accent color for links, highlights, tags, and special promotions.

### 2.2 Supporting Neutrals (Recommended)

To give the UI and print materials more flexibility, introduce a few neutrals:

- **Soft Ink**: `#333333`  
  - **Usage**: Default body text (instead of pure black in most cases; reduces eye strain).
- **Warm Grey**: `#B5B0C5`  
  - **Usage**: Borders, dividers, secondary text, placeholders.
- **Off-White**: `#FAF9FD`  
  - **Usage**: Panels, cards, elevated surfaces against the main light background.

### 2.3 Functional Color Roles

- **Primary action (CTA)**:
  - Fill: Oh My Brew Red (`#AC0000`)
  - Text: White (`#FFFFFF`)
- **Secondary action**:
  - Fill: Transparent or Off-White (`#FAF9FD`)
  - Border: Red (`#AC0000`)
  - Text: Red (`#AC0000`)
- **Links**:
  - Default: Electric Brew Blue (`#1D0FCB`)
  - Hover: Slightly lighter blue or an underline.
- **Status colors (suggested)**:
  - Success: `#2E7D32`
  - Warning: `#F9A825`
  - Error: Use red (`#AC0000`), but sparingly and with clear context.

## 3. Typography

### 3.1 Type System Overview

Aim for a combination of the following fonts:

- **Display / Handwritten**: `Pacifico` for headings and logo-like text, echoing the brand’s playful, curved personality.
- **Body / UI Sans-Serif**: `Nunito` for UI, menus, and long text, providing clean, friendly legibility.

You can plug in specific fonts later; the guidelines below apply regardless of exact font choices.

### 3.2 Recommended Font Roles

- **Display Font (Brand / Headings)**  
  - Font: `Pacifico`.  
  - Style: Rounded, slightly irregular, hand-lettered or brush-like.  
  - Usage: Logo, main headings (H1/H2), large promos, signage.
- **Body Font (UI / Text)**  
  - Font: `Nunito`.  
  - Style: Modern, humanist sans-serif.  
  - Usage: Paragraphs, labels, navigation, buttons, menu descriptions.

### 3.3 Typographic Scale (Web/App)

Use a comfortable, coffee-shop-like reading scale:

- **H1**: 32–40 px, bold, red (`#AC0000`) or black on light background.  
- **H2**: 24–28 px, medium/bold.  
- **H3**: 20–22 px, medium.  
- **Body**: 16 px regular (line-height 1.5–1.7).  
- **Caption / Meta**: 12–14 px, lighter neutral.

### 3.4 Styling Rules

- **Headings**:
  - Prefer red headings for brand-forward pages (home, marketing).
  - Use black/dark headings for dense informational content.
- **Body text**:
  - Default color: Soft Ink (`#333333`).
  - Maintain comfortable line length (60–80 characters).
- **Emphasis**:
  - Use **bold** rather than underlines for emphasis in body copy.
  - Reserve Electric Brew Blue (`#1D0FCB`) primarily for links, not general emphasis.

## 4. Illustration, Iconography & Imagery

### 4.1 Cat Character

- **Role**: Signature brand mascot—curious, playful, sometimes “clumsy” in a cute way.
- **Style**:
  - Simple silhouette or minimal-detail illustration in black.
  - Rounded edges, expressive but not overly detailed features.
- **Usage**:
  - Integrate the cat with typography (hanging from letters, peeking from corners).
  - Use sparingly in UI (empty states, loaders, error pages) so it feels special.

### 4.2 Illustrations

- **Style**:
  - Flat or minimal shading, high contrast.
  - Rounded corners, organic shapes that feel hand-drawn rather than geometric.
- **Colors**:
  - Keep illustrations within the core palette (red, light background, black, blue, plus neutrals).
  - Avoid overly bright or neon colors that clash with the cozy, warm brand.

### 4.3 Icons

- **Stroke style**: Simple, monoline or slightly thick line; rounded corners.
- **Color**:
  - Default: Black (`#000000`) or Soft Ink (`#333333`) on light background.
  - Active/selected: Red (`#AC0000`) or Blue (`#1D0FCB`), depending on context.
- **Consistency**:
  - All icons should share a consistent visual language (line thickness, corner radius, level of detail).

### 4.4 Photography (If Used)

- **Tone**:
  - Warm, cozy café atmosphere—wood tones, soft lighting, steam, ceramics.
- **Composition**:
  - Keep backgrounds simple and not too busy so UI overlays and text remain legible.
- **Color grading**:
  - Subtle warmth; avoid high saturation. Photos should harmonize with the light background and red accents.

## 5. Layout & UI Principles

### 5.1 General Layout

- **Feel**: Airy, uncluttered, with plenty of white (light) space.
- **Grid**:
  - Use a simple grid (for example, 12-column for web, 4/8-point spacing system for app).
- **Alignment**:
  - Prefer left-aligned text for readability.
  - Center alignment reserved for hero sections or short headlines.

### 5.2 Spacing & Corners

- **Spacing system**:
  - Small: 4 px, 8 px  
  - Medium: 12 px, 16 px  
  - Large: 24 px, 32 px  
- **Corners**:
  - Use soft, rounded corners on buttons and cards (for example, 8–12 px radius) to echo the organic, friendly brand.

### 5.3 Hierarchy

- **Primary focus**:
  - Key actions (for example, `Order Now`, `Subscribe`, `View Menu`) in red buttons.
- **Secondary info**:
  - Use muted text, smaller type, or grey tones.
- **Use of color**:
  - Red is for key brand actions, urgent highlights, and core elements—avoid using it for long passages of text.

## 6. Buttons, Forms & Components

### 6.1 Buttons

- **Primary Button**:
  - Fill: `#AC0000`
  - Text: `#FFFFFF`
  - Shape: Rounded (8–12 px radius)
  - Hover: Slightly darker red or subtle scaling; maintain text color.
- **Secondary Button**:
  - Border: `#AC0000`
  - Background: Transparent or `#FAF9FD`
  - Text: `#AC0000`
- **Tertiary / Text Button**:
  - Text: `#1D0FCB`
  - No border, minimal padding, for subtle actions.

### 6.2 Forms

- **Input fields**:
  - Background: `#FAF9FD` or `#FFFFFF`
  - Border: `#B5B0C5`
  - Focus: Border or outline in `#1D0FCB` (blue) to distinguish from red error states.
- **Error states**:
  - Border: `#AC0000`
  - Text: Red error message, but short and friendly.
- **Labels & help text**:
  - Label: `#333333`, 14 px.
  - Helper text: `#B5B0C5`, 12–14 px.

## 7. Brand Voice & Copy Guidelines

### 7.1 Tone of Voice

- **Core traits**:
  - Warm, playful, and friendly.
  - Confident in coffee quality, but never snobbish.
  - Slightly cheeky or humorous when appropriate.
- **Examples**:
  - Instead of `Submit your order`, use `Brew it!`
  - Instead of `Error: invalid email`, use `Hmm… that email doesn’t look right yet.`

### 7.2 Writing Style

- Use short, clear sentences.
- Address the user directly (`your brew`, `your order`).
- Sprinkle light coffee metaphors, but don’t overdo it.

---

## 8. Accessibility & Contrast

### 8.1 Contrast

- Ensure sufficient contrast between text and background:
  - Dark text (`#333333` or `#000000`) on `#F2F0F6` is acceptable.
  - When placing red or blue text on light backgrounds, test contrast against WCAG guidelines, especially for small text.
- For critical information (prices, buttons, labels), prioritize legibility over stylistic color choices.

### 8.2 Interaction States

- Provide clear hover, focus, and active states:
  - Buttons: Color or subtle shadow change.
  - Links: Underline on hover.
  - Inputs: Visible focus ring in blue (`#1D0FCB`).

### 8.3 Typography

- Maintain minimum text size of **14 px** for body content whenever possible.
- Avoid long stretches of all-caps; reserve for short labels or badges.

## 9. Applications & Examples

### 9.1 Coffee Packaging

- **Front**:
  - Logo centered or top-left on light background.
  - Red headline (for example, `Espresso Blend`) and black supporting text.
- **Back or Side**:
  - Use a limited color palette: red accents, black text, and light background.
  - Consider a small cat illustration as a signature touch.

### 9.2 Menus & Signage

- Background: `#F2F0F6` or off-white.
- Headings in red; item names in black; descriptions in Soft Ink (`#333333`).
- Divider lines or sections in Warm Grey (`#B5B0C5`).

### 9.3 Digital (Website / App)

- **Hero section**:
  - Light background, bold red heading, black or dark body copy.
  - Primary CTA in red.
- **Sections**:
  - Alternate lightly tinted backgrounds (for example, `#F2F0F6` / `#FAF9FD`) to create depth.
  - Consistent spacing and clear hierarchy.

  - Always consult this guide before creating new visuals.
  - If new colors or fonts are needed, derive them thoughtfully from existing hues and typographic styles.

## 11. Dev-ready Design System Primitives

This section defines **low-level, reusable components** that implement the design tokens and guidelines above. Use these primitives to build any page; keep page-specific layouts and flows documented elsewhere.

All examples assume Tailwind CSS with design tokens wired via CSS variables (for example, `bg-background`, `text-foreground`, `bg-primary`, `border-border`).

### 11.1 Typography

- **Text** (single typography primitive)
  - **Purpose**: One unified text component used for headings, body copy, labels, captions, and “eyebrow” text.
  - **Core ideas**:
    - **Semantic element** (`as`): choose the underlying tag (`h1`, `h2`, `p`, `span`, `small`, `label`, etc.) based on document structure and accessibility.
    - **Variant** (role): communicates intent such as `heading`, `body`, `label`, `caption`, `eyebrow`.
    - **Size**: maps to the typographic scale from section 3.3 (for example `xl`, `lg`, `md`, `sm`, `xs`).
    - **Tone** (color role): ties into the color system, for example `default` (Soft Ink), `muted` (Warm Grey), `brand` (Oh My Brew Red), `link` (Electric Brew Blue), `success`, `danger`.
    - **Align**: `left`, `center`, or `right`, with left-aligned as the default for readability.

### 11.2 Buttons & CTAs

- **Button** (single button primitive)
  - **Purpose**: Unified button component used for primary, secondary, and text/link-style actions.
  - **Core ideas**:
    - **`variant`**: expresses intent such as `primary`, `secondary`, `ghost`, or `link`.
    - **`size`**: controls padding and font size (for example `lg`, `md`, `sm`).
    - **`tone`** (optional): allows mapping to semantic meanings like `default`, `danger`, or `success` if needed.
    - **`fullWidth`** (optional): stretches the button to the container width for mobile-heavy layouts.
  - **Styling guidance** (from section 6.1):
    - **Primary (`variant="primary"`)**:
      - Background: `bg-primary` (`#AC0000`), text: `text-primary-foreground` (`#FFFFFF`).
      - Shape: rounded (`rounded-lg` to `rounded-xl`, 8–12 px radius).
      - Hover: slightly darker red or subtle scale (`hover:brightness-95` or `hover:-translate-y-[1px]`) while preserving contrast.
    - **Secondary (`variant="secondary"`)**:
      - Background: transparent or Off-White (`bg-secondary`).
      - Border: `border border-primary`.
      - Text: `text-primary`.
      - Hover: light background fill (`hover:bg-secondary/80`).
    - **Link (`variant="link"`)**:
      - Text: Electric Brew Blue (`text-[color:var(--color-omb-electric-brew-blue)]` or `text-accent`).
      - Underline or color change on hover (`hover:underline`), minimal padding and no border.

### 11.3 Form Primitives

These primitives are intended for any form, not just a specific page.

- **FieldLabel**
  - **Purpose**: Label for inputs, textareas, and similar controls.
  - **Style**:
    - Uses the `Text` primitive with a label-style preset (for example, `variant="label"`).
    - Size: 14 px (`text-sm`), aligned with form controls.

- **TextInput**
  - **Purpose**: Single-line text entry (for example, email, name).
  - **Style** (from 6.2):
    - Background: `bg-secondary` or white (`bg-white`) as needed.
    - Border: `border border-border` (Warm Grey).
    - Radius: align with buttons (8–12 px).
    - Focus: border/ring in Electric Brew Blue (`focus:ring-2 focus:ring-ring focus:border-ring`).
    - Error state: border in Oh My Brew Red (`border-[color:var(--color-status-error)]`) with a clear, friendly error message.

- **FormHelperText**
  - **Purpose**: Small hint or validation message below inputs.
  - **Style**:
    - Uses the `Text` primitive with `variant="caption"` and a muted tone.
    - Typography: 12–14 px (`text-xs` / `text-sm`).

### 11.4 Status & Badges

- **StatusPill**
  - **Purpose**: Small pill/badge for statuses (for example, “Coming soon”, “New”, “Limited”).
  - **Style**:
    - Shape: fully rounded (`rounded-full`), small padding (`px-3 py-1`).
    - Default colors: Off-White background (`bg-secondary`) with Oh My Brew Red text (`text-primary`).
    - Success variant: `bg-[var(--color-status-success)]` with white text.
  - **Copy**:
    - Keep text short and friendly, consistent with the brand voice.
